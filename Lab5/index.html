New Study Finds Americans Scoot Over At Least 10 Miles Per Year

WASHINGTON, DC. A new report from the Department of Transportation claims the average American travels about 10 miles per year doing one simple thing: scooting over to make room for someone else.

Researchers tracked movement on benches, bleachers, couches, and backseats and found that small slides, scooches, and awkward shuffles add up fast when you repeat them every day.

The study also noted that people often scoot over, realize there was already enough space, and then scoot back, doubling the distance for no reason except social panic.

Black Guy Asks Nation For Change

CHICAGO. Witnesses say a loud man approached a crowd of thousands in downtown Chicago and repeatedly demanded change from everyone who would listen.

“The time for change is now,” the man shouted, speaking for nearly twenty minutes straight while urging strangers to finally deliver the change he needed.

He told the crowd the need for change was urgent and insisted that only the people standing there could make change happen, right now, in this very moment.

One local salesman said he had already heard the same speech multiple times that day and could not understand why the man was so obsessed with change or what he planned to do with it.

After his opening lines, the man shifted topics constantly, jumping from affordable health care to foreign policy to jobs, then circling back to his original request for change like it was the only thing that mattered.

Reports say the man has traveled from city to city doing the same routine, sometimes hugging strangers, sometimes pointing at random people and begging for help, and sometimes getting angry when he did not get the change he wanted.

Most bystanders ignored him, though a few admitted they felt slightly guilty, like they should help, even if they had no idea what kind of change he actually meant.

A local gun shop owner said he works hard, pays taxes, and does not appreciate being approached by a stranger demanding change, adding that what the man really needs is a job.

The Onion March 19, 2008

Wealthy Teen Nearly Experiences Consequence

SOMERSET, NJ. Authorities described a “near tragedy” this weekend after a wealthy teenager came dangerously close to facing a real consequence for something he did.

Police reports say the teen drank at a party, ignored friends who told him not to drive, and then got behind the wheel anyway because he felt completely untouchable.

While driving home, he drifted into other lanes and slammed into a minivan carrying a family, briefly putting him face to face with the kind of moment that usually teaches people a lesson.

That lesson did not arrive, however, because the teen’s airbags deployed and his family’s lawyers allegedly arrived so fast that they might have been riding in the trunk.

The police chief stated the teen showed no remorse, claimed he did nothing wrong, and somehow avoided any serious punishment despite clearly being responsible.

One officer said the teen spent most of the night repeating, “Do you know who I am,” as if his last name alone should override traffic laws.

The teen’s father told reporters his son is “strong” for refusing to accept blame, adding that admitting fault would have been “a sign of weakness.”

A family friend who works in the legal system called the situation a miracle, explaining that the teen survived the crash with no injuries, no responsibility, and the exact same personality.

In the days after the crash, the teen was reportedly almost assigned community service, but the punishment was reduced after testimony focused on how emotionally difficult it was for him to be mildly inconvenienced.

During the process, the teen was forced to pause a video game for a few seconds to sign paperwork, an event his family later described as “traumatizing.”

The teen later said it was terrifying to briefly imagine a world where actions have outcomes, admitting he almost learned something.

At press time, the teen was relaxing comfortably at home while the victims remained hospitalized, and sources confirmed he was already back to living like nothing happened.
